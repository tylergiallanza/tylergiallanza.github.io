About

I am currently a PhD Student in Psychology and Neuroscience at Princeton University, advised by Jonathan Cohen. I'm broadly interested in understanding the computational foundations of human cognitive processes like learning and cognitive control, as well as leveraging that understanding to develop better automated agents.

I received my undergraduate degree in Computer Science at Southern Methodist University, where I applied deep learning to problems in cybersecurity with Mitchell Thorton and Eric Larson at the Darwin Deason InstituteÂ for Cyber Security. I also spent a year as a visiting student at the University of Oxford studying formal models of computation.
